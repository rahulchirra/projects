Essential Hygiene Guidance

Daily Cleansing

Gently wash the vulva with warm water only. Avoid scented soaps, douches, and harsh cleansers that can disrupt pH balance.

Expert Tip:

Always wipe from front to back after using the toilet to prevent bacterial transfer.

Period Hygiene

Change pads/tampons every 4-6 hours. Consider menstrual cups for eco-friendly protection. Wash hands before and after changing products.

Expert Tip:

At night, use overnight pads or period underwear for better protection and comfort.

Infection Prevention

Wear cotton underwear, avoid tight clothing, and change out of wet swimsuits/exercise clothes promptly to prevent yeast infections.

Expert Tip:

Probiotic-rich foods like yogurt can help maintain healthy vaginal flora.

Sleep Hygiene

Sleep without underwear when possible to allow air circulation. Choose loose, breathable pajamas made from natural fabrics.

Expert Tip:

A regular sleep schedule helps regulate hormones that affect vaginal health.

Post-Intimacy Care

Urinate after sex to flush bacteria from the urethra. Clean the genital area with warm water and pat dry gently.

Expert Tip:

Avoid scented lubricants which can cause irritation - opt for water-based, pH-balanced products.

Symptom Awareness

Be alert for unusual discharge, odor, itching or pain - these may indicate infection requiring medical attention.

Expert Tip:

Track symptoms in our app to identify patterns and discuss with your healthcare provider.

Cycle-Synced Nutrition

Foods for Each Phase

Menstrual Phase (Days 1-5)

Focus on iron-rich foods to replenish what's lost during bleeding:

Leafy greens Red meat Lentils Dark chocolate Pumpkin seeds

Follicular Phase (Days 6-14)

Support estrogen production with these foods:

Flaxseeds Cruciferous veggies Fermented foods Berries Whole grains

Ovulation Phase (Days 15-17)

Boost energy and support ovulation with:

Avocados Nuts Salmon Quinoa Asparagus

Luteal Phase (Days 18-28)

Combat PMS and support progesterone with:

Sweet potatoes Bananas Dark leafy greens Turkey Chamomile tea

Daily Hydration & Wellness

Hydration Essentials

Proper hydration is crucial for all aspects of women's health, from circulation to vaginal moisture balance.

Aim for 2-3 liters of water daily
Add lemon or cucumber for flavor and extra benefits
Herbal teas count toward hydration (peppermint, ginger, chamomile)

Foods to Avoid

Certain foods can exacerbate period symptoms and affect vaginal health:

Excess caffeine (increases anxiety and breast tenderness)
Alcohol (disrupts hormone balance)
Processed sugars (worsens inflammation and cramps)
Salty foods (contributes to bloating)
